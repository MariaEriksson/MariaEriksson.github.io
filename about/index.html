    I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at The Hospital for Sick Children (Toronto, Canada), funded by the Sigrid Jusélius Stiftelse. My work focuses on clinical and cognitive outcomes after epilepsy surgery in children.

    I obtained my PhD at UCL Institute of Child Health and Great Ormond Street Hospital in 2023, under the supervision of Professor Torsten Baldeweg, Professor Helen Cross, and Mr Martin Tisdall. My PhD was funded by the NIHR Great Ormond Street Hospital Biomedical Research Centre as part of the UCL Child Health Research PhD Programme.

    In 2020, I was awarded the UCL Research Scholarship for Cross-Disciplinary Training to undertake an additional year of training at the Alan Turing Institute, the UK’s national institute for data science and machine learning. Here, under the supervision of Dr Kirstie Whitaker, I learnt how to apply an open and reproducible science approach to clinical research.
