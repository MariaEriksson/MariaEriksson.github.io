    I am a PhD student based jointly at UCL Institute of Child Health and Great Ormond Street Hospital. I am supervised by Professor Torsten Baldeweg and Professor Helen Cross. My PhD is funded by NIHR Great Ormond Street Hospital Biomedical Research Centre as part of the UCL Child Health Research PhD Programme.

    My research aims to predict clinical and cognitive outcomes after paediatric epilepsy surgery to guide surgical candidate selection, inform post-surgical rehabilitation, and provide family counselling. I use large sets of electronic medical records and neuroimaging data, alongside data science and machine learning, to achieve a precision-medicine approach for children with epilepsy.

    In October 2020, I was awarded the UCL Research Scholarship for Cross-Disciplinary Training to undertake an additional year of training at the Alan Turing Institute, the UK’s national institute for data science and machine learning. Here, under the supervision of Dr Kirstie Whitaker, I am learning how to apply an open and reproducible science approach to clinical research.
